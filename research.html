Research interests

My research interests are physical processes of planets, stars and disks. For example, how planets and stars form and evolve, how planetary and stellar magnetic fields are generated and evolve, how tidal waves in binary system propagate and dissipate, and why protoplanetary and black hole disks become unstable, etc. To understand these phenomena, knowledges of orbital dynamics, thermodynamics, fluid dynamics and electrodynamics are needed, and mathematical models are built and studied by means of order-of-magnitude estimations and paper-and-pencil calculations with a little numerical computations. Below are some objects that I study. From left to right: Earth's magnetic fields, Jupiter's red spot, stellar interior, tides in binary stars, and accretion disk.
